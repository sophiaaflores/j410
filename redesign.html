The Story

If you're an incoming freshman at Cal Poly, figuring out housing can feel overwhelming as you prepare to leave for college. You're asked to find a roommate, choose a living community, sort through different room types, and make sense of the costs all at once.

Where you live will shape not just your space, but your first-year experience and how you adjust to this new chapter. As a first-year student at Cal Poly, you'll live in one of six housing communities, and within each, you'll be placed into a Residential Learning Community — where you'll live alongside students who share similar interests, goals, or backgrounds.

You can rank your top choices, but with a variety of options, it's not exactly easy to picture what daily life looks like.

This website is built to help you understand your options more clearly — breaking down Cal Poly's living communities using real data, student perspectives, and key details — so you can make a confident choice about where you'll call home.

Where You'll Live

The Six Communities

As a first-year student at Cal Poly, you'll live in one of six housing communities. Here's what makes each one unique — tap any card to learn more.

01
yakʔitʸutʸu
Newest Community

Cal Poly's newest and most modern community. Seven halls named after Northern Chumash villages, built with sustainability in mind and LEED Gold certified.

Triples & QuintuplesLEED GoldCafé on-site
Modern · Social · Sustainable
Features an amphitheater, outdoor courtyards, and murals throughout. The café and common spaces make it one of the most community-oriented options for first-years.
02
Sierra Madre
The Towers

A mix of students from all majors housed in concrete towers. Close to the campus core with a community center and front desk.

All MajorsCommunity CenterCentral Location
Diverse · Central · Connected
Sierra Madre draws students from every college and major, making it one of the most socially diverse communities on campus.
03
Yosemite
The Ten Towers

Ten towers numbered 0–9, co-ed floors, and a large main hall with a pool table, ping pong, and study lounge. Laundry available in even-numbered towers.

10 TowersCo-ed FloorsGame Room
Lively · Active · Community
One of the larger communities on campus. The main hall serves as a central social hub — pool table, ping pong, and study spaces all in one place.
04
South Mountain
The Red Bricks

The closest community to the campus core. Six halls with a game room, laundry, study lounge, and community kitchen. Doubles and triples available.

Closest to CampusGame RoomCommunity Kitchen
Convenient · Classic · Central
Known as the Red Bricks for their iconic architecture, South Mountain buildings are the most centrally located on campus.
05
North Mountain
The Five Halls

Five halls — Shasta, Diablo, Palomar, Whitney, and Lassen. Triple rooms with community-style bathrooms and access to South Mountain's Santa Lucia amenities.

5 HallsTriple RoomsShared Amenities
Quiet · Close-Knit · Scenic
Situated slightly away from the campus core, North Mountain offers a quieter living experience with access to Santa Lucia hall amenities.
06
Poly Canyon Village
PCV

Hillside apartments with a full kitchen, living room, two bathrooms, and 4–5 bedrooms per unit. Pool, basketball, and volleyball courts on-site.

Full Kitchen4–5 BedroomsPool & Courts
Independent · Spacious · Social
The most apartment-like option for first-years. PCV gives students more independence with full kitchens and living rooms, while still being on campus.
07 Opening Fall 2026
Building A
New Housing · South Mountain Area

Cal Poly's brand-new modular residence hall opening fall 2026, located behind Trinity and Muir Hall in the Red Bricks area. Houses just over 500 students in suite-style rooms.

~500 StudentsSuite-Style RoomsShared Living SpaceStudy LoungesCommunity Kitchens
New · Modern · Suite-Style
Part of Cal Poly's $1.2 billion 10-year housing expansion plan. Suites feature shared bedrooms, bathrooms, and a shared living space. Community commons, quiet study areas, and kitchens are located throughout the building. Built using innovative modular construction — the first large-scale use of modular construction in the CSU system.
